The Interactivity module allows adding different types of interactive annotations to videos. The Lead Form Annotation will enable you to embed a form from systems like Eloqua, Salesforce, Marketo, and HubSpot.

Before you get started

Before you can add interactivity to a player, you need an Interactivity-enabled Brightcove Player and you need to make sure an Interactivity project has been created. See Getting Started with the Interactivity Module and Configuring a Brightcove Player with the Interactivity Plugin for more information.

IMPORTANT: Before you start, in the Players module > Interactivity, you must enable the Version 2 of the plugin on the Options(JSON):

"enablePluginV2": true

Adding a Lead Form to a video

Follow these steps to add a Lead Form to a video:

  1. Login to Video Cloud Studio.
  2. Open the Interactivity module.
  3. Click New project. Or you can click on a project already created.
  4. Enter a Project Title.
  5. Select a video to use.
  6. Click Create.
  7. Play the video to the point where the Lead Form should be displayed.
  8. In the bottom toolbar, click on the Lead Form icon.
  9. Click on the Edit link in the menu.
  10. Click on the Form source tab to add the Lead Form Code. On this screen, you can also click on the options Require submission and Show form everytime. You should see something similar to this:

    In the case where you are working with Microsoft or Google Forms, you should see something like this:

    IMPORTANT: Notice that in Google and Microsoft Forms, the options Require submission and Show form everytime are NOT available. You need to click the X to exit.

  11. Click on the Time tab to enter the start time.
  12. Click Done to finish or Delete to delete the annotation.
  13. Click on the Resize link in the menu to drag the Lead Form to where you want it to appear on the video.
    Note: Make sure the Lead Form isn't placed at the bottom of the player where it may be covered up by the control bar.
  14. You can also click to edit, or click to Duplicate, Delete, or Edit the annotation in the section under the player preview:
  15. Click Embed.
  16. Click Preview, play the video and confirm that the Lead Form appears.

Adding a Lead Form as a template

Follow these steps to add a Lead Form as a template:

Note: It is necessary to set up the following property to auto-create Interactivity projects to add templates by tag:


      {
        "create": true
      }

For more information about this setting, see Configuring a Brightcove Player with the Interactivity Plugin.

  1. Login to Video Cloud Studio.
  2. Open the Interactivity module.
  3. In the left navigation, click Templates > All templates.
  4. Click New Template.
  5. Enter a Template Title.

  6. Click Create.

    Brightcove Interactivity automatically loads a one-minute sample video source. If the Lead Form will appear at the beginning of every video or within one minute of playback, continue with the existing sample video. If it will appear at a specific time above 60 seconds (such as 5 minutes into a video), click Replace Video to select a video of the correct length to create your template.
  7. Play the video to the point where the Lead Form should be displayed.
  8. In the bottom toolbar, click on the Lead Form icon.
  9. Click on the Edit link in the menu.
  10. Click on the Form source tab to add the Lead Form Code. On this screen, you can also click on the options Require submission and Show form everytime. You should see something similar to this:
  11. Click on the Time tab to enter the start time.
  12. Click Done to finish or Delete to delete the annotation.
  13. Click on the Resize link in the menu to drag the Lead Form to where you want it to appear on the video.
    Note: Make sure the Lead Form isn't placed at the bottom of the player where it may be covered up by the control bar.
  14. You can also click to edit, or click to Duplicate, Delete, or Edit the annotation in the section under the player preview:
  15. To apply the Lead Form Template across multiple projects or videos, click Settings.
  16. Use the Apply Templates to Projects by Tag input to define a unique tag. The tag will be assigned to the videos in the Media Module. Publish the video using a player and confirm that the Lead Form appears.
    If you have projects based on your videos in the Interactivity Module, you can add the tag defined previously to these Projects. The lead form should appear in the Interactivity Player (Make sure the corresponding video has the tag in the Media Module). An Interactivity Project will be generated automatically from the video that contains its tags, and the Lead Form Template will automatically be applied upon playback.

Known Issues

  • Currently, the source for the Lead Form Annotation will be cached. This means that after you update the source, you may not be able to see your changes immediately. Therefore, if you want to update the Lead Form Annotation source to something different and expect those changes to have an immediate effect, we recommend removing the existing Lead Form Annotation and creating a new one.
